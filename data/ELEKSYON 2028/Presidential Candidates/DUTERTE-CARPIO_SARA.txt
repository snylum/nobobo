Full Name: Sara Zimmerman Duterte-Carpio
Nickname: Inday Sara
Position: Vice President of the Philippines (current, under suspension pending impeachment trial)
Party: Hugpong ng Pagbabago (HNP) / previously allied with PDP-Laban

--- BACKGROUND ---
Born: May 31, 1978, Davao City
Education: AB Political Science, University of the Philippines Diliman;
           LLB, San Beda College of Law
Profession: Lawyer, Politician
Family: Daughter of former President Rodrigo Duterte; married to Manases Carpio

--- POSITIONS HELD ---
- Vice President of the Philippines (2022–present)
- Secretary of Education (2022–2024, resigned)
- Mayor of Davao City (2010–2013, 2016–2022)
- Representative, 1st District of Davao City (2013–2016)

--- TRACK RECORD AS VP / EDUCATION SECRETARY ---
- Oversaw DepEd during post-pandemic school reopening
- Pushed for "Matatag" curriculum reform to reduce academic burden
- Resigned as Education Secretary in June 2024 amid political rift with Marcos administration
- Declared presidential bid for 2028

--- LEGISLATION / BILLS AUTHORED ---
- As Mayor: spearheaded Davao City's anti-drug and peace and order programs
- Endorsed Anti-Online Sexual Abuse and Exploitation of Children (OSAEC) measures
- Supported Bayanihan recovery legislation during COVID-19

--- IMPEACHMENT RECORD ---
First Impeachment (2025):
- Impeached by the House of Representatives on February 5, 2025 (215–0 vote)
- Articles included: misuse of confidential funds, unexplained bank transactions,
  death threats against President Marcos, First Lady, and House Speaker Romualdez
- Senate remanded articles back to the House on June 10, 2025
- Supreme Court nullified the complaint on July 25, 2025 (Duterte v. House of Representatives,
  G.R. No. 278353), ruling it unconstitutional due to the one-year bar rule

Second Impeachment (2026):
- New complaints filed starting February 2, 2026 after one-year ban lapsed
- House Committee on Justice voted unanimously (53–0) to find probable cause on April 29, 2026
- House plenary voted 257–26 (9 abstentions) to impeach on May 11, 2026
- Articles include: unexplained wealth (bank transactions inconsistent with SALN),
  misuse of public/confidential funds, death threats against top officials
- As of May 2026, impeachment trial in Senate is uncertain following Senate
  leadership change; Alan Cayetano elected as new Senate President (Duterte ally)

--- SALN / WEALTH ---
- Bank transaction records flagged by AMLC showed billions in transactions
  inconsistent with her declared assets
- AMLC confirmed 18 out of 18 transactions identified by complainants were
  in their records (as of 2026 hearings)
- Husband Manases Carpio filed criminal complaints against House legislators
  citing violation of bank secrecy laws after records were made public

--- CONTROVERSIES ---
- Death threat controversy (2024): During an online press conference, threatened
  to have President Marcos, First Lady Liza Marcos, and Speaker Romualdez killed
  by an assassin if she herself was assassinated; later said the threat "was not a joke"
- Alleged misuse of confidential funds as VP and as DepEd Secretary
- Father Rodrigo Duterte arrested by ICC order in 2025; she accused Marcos of
  allowing the "kidnapping" of her father in violation of Philippine laws
- Alleged links to Davao Death Squad (cited in first impeachment articles)

--- POLITICAL ALLIANCES ---
- Ran as Marcos's running mate in 2022 under a joint ticket
- Political alliance with Marcos collapsed publicly by late 2023
- Now considered a leading opposition figure and 2028 presidential aspirant
- Father's arrest and her impeachment seen as part of broader Marcos-Duterte feud
